I am currently a research scientist at FeatureX working on machine learning and deep learning for semantic analysis of satellite images. Being part of the FeatureX team, we are on the quest accessorizing advanced machine learning systems to the public, and transforming the remote sensing industry as a whole.

Prior to joining FeatureX, I was a research assistant at MIT Media Lab, and before that, at National Taiwan University. I worked with Professor Deb Roy and Shou-De Lin, respectively. As a research assistant, the research projects revolved around building machine learning systems that help us better understand the network society that we live in. I have worked on social media analytics, recommendation systems, and sentiment analysis.

I am intensely interested in making artificial neural nets sense and reason what they see just as a human would. Many of my current work / side projects revolve around learning generalizable vision.

    Learning Rotationally Invariant Features

    FeatureX I worked on designing a novel network architecture that achieves rotational invariance for the network features. This approach can help generalization, since satellite images are taken overhead, disregarding object orientation. ...more

    Analyzing and Characterizing Bursts on Twitter

    MIT I developed an approach that considers both interaction dynamics and content semantics for analyzing characteristics of bursty events on Twitter. The proposed approach can help qualitatively understand how a social media reaction is attribued to content or the user herself.

    Analyzing Journalist Activities through Their Social Media Presence

    MIT As part of the team, I helped build an end-to-end analytic system for mapping network dynamics among journalists. I further explored ways to use Bayesian topic modeling for analyzing effects of social features on news content report.

    Embedding Social Media Discussions

    MIT I explored how 2016 election discussions through the lens of word embedding. By mapping the tweets to embedding space and group terms by semantic similarity / user interactions. We discovered significant topic influence on tweeting behaviors. The same approach can also be used for mapping topic discussions geographically.

    Tool for mapping lack of news coverage for local communities

    MIT I built a visualization tool that runs geographically weighted regression to map spatial correlation between demographic attributes and crime rate. For News and Participatory Media course. website

    Heterogeneous Network Sampling

    NTU We demonstrated a budget-aware method to sample a heterogeneous social network and show that such sampled network can be used to trained effective node/link-type classifiers.

    Unsupervised Link Prediction and Preference Propagation in Heterogeneous Social Networks

    NTU We explored modeling how preference (represented as a vector) propagates through a network. We also proposed an unsupervised link prediction model through message passing in a factor graph that considers local/global social structures.

    Ad click-through rate prediction

    NTU Our team did extensive feature engineering, and in addition incorporated a novel two-stage model ensemble, winning KDDCup 2012.

    Time series nearest neighbor search in distributed sensor networks

    NTU We explored ways to do efficient kNN search in sensor network subjected to bandwidth constraint. Proposed a tightening algorithm to reduce data transmission during searching.

    Action recognition / classification for body sensor networks

    NTU Our team tackled traditional machine learning approaches for recognizing a variety of actions in human activities. Won 2nd place in Body Sensor Network Challenge.
